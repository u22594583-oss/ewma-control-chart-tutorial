MEWMA Control Chart Explorer

A hands-on companion to MEWMA_Control_Chart_Tutorial.ipynb. Turn the knobs below to see how the smoothing parameter λ, the correlation ρ between two monitored variables, and the size/direction of a process shift interact to determine how quickly the MEWMA chart signals — and why a shift's direction relative to the correlation matters just as much as its size. Fixed at p = 2 variables so the geometry stays visible; the notebook's math generalizes to any number of variables.

The formulas behind the knobs

Observations Xi ~ N2(μ0, Σ) in control, with Σ = [[1,ρ],[ρ,1]] (unit variances, so ρ alone sets the correlation knob). After the shift point, the mean moves to μ0 + (c·cosθ, c·sinθ).

MEWMA recursion: Zi = λXi + (1−λ)Zi−1, Z0 = μ0.

Asymptotic covariance: ΣZ = [λ/(2−λ)] Σ (steady-state limit, the standard convention for ARL studies — matching the notebook's Section 9–10).

Chart statistic: T2i = (Zi−μ0)′ ΣZ−1 (Zi−μ0). A signal occurs the first time T2i > h.

"Auto-tune h" runs an in-browser Monte Carlo bisection search (same method as the notebook's Section 9): simulate many in-control runs at a candidate h, measure the average run length (ARL0), and adjust h until that average hits your target.

Because Σ has equal variances, its eigenvectors are fixed at ±45° regardless of ρ (eigenvalues 1±ρ) — which is what lets both ellipses below be drawn from a closed form rather than a general eigensolver, and also *why* a shift along the (45°) eigenvector direction is easiest to hide from the raw axes but a shift along (−45°, against positive correlation) is the fastest one to catch.

Companion to MEWMA_Control_Chart_Tutorial.ipynb in this repository. The MEWMA chart originates with Lowry, Woodall, Champ & Rigdon (1992), A Multivariate Exponentially Weighted Moving Average Control Chart, Technometrics 34(1). Runs entirely client-side — no data leaves your browser.
